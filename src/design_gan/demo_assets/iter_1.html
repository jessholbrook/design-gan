About the Tour

Vermont in full color, from the saddle.

Green Mountain Gravel is an intimate, fully supported weekend tour designed for riders who want to experience Vermont at its most spectacular — late September peak foliage — at a pace that lets you actually take it in.

We keep the group small (40 riders max) so rest stops feel like gatherings, not checkpoints. Expect local farm breakfasts, afternoon cider tastings, and an inn stay in the heart of Woodstock.

The Route

62 miles. Two wildly different days.

Day one rolls through covered bridges and river valleys. Day two climbs into the hills for the views that make Vermont famous. Both days end with a reason to celebrate.

Day 1 — Saturday, Sept 20

River Valleys & Covered Bridges

↔ 62 miles ↑ 2,100 ft gain ⏱ Est. 4–6 hrs

Start at the Woodstock green and follow the Ottauquechee River east toward Quechee Gorge, then loop back through farm roads and six historic covered bridges. Terrain is rolling with no sustained climbs — perfect for settling in.

  • Woodstock Village Green (start/finish)
  • Quechee Gorge overlook
  • Middle Bridge & Taftsville Covered Bridge
  • Billings Farm lunch stop
  • Suicide Six valley loop
  • Evening dinner at Simon Pearce

Day 2 — Sunday, Sept 21

The Green Mountain Climb

↔ 62 miles ↑ 2,700 ft gain ⏱ Est. 5–7 hrs

The signature day. Head northwest on Route 12, climb Barnard Gulf Road through dense forest canopy, crest the ridge at 1,800 ft for panoramic valley views, then descend to Bethel for a cider stop before looping home.

  • Inn departure & farm breakfast
  • Barnard Gulf Road — the big climb
  • Ridge summit viewpoint (1,820 ft)
  • Silver Lake optional swim stop
  • Bethel cider tasting at Windfall Orchard
  • Return to Woodstock — farewell gathering

FAQ

Common questions.

What fitness level is this tour designed for? ▾
This tour suits riders who can comfortably complete a 50–70 mile day with 2,000–3,000 ft of climbing. You don't need to race — but you should be comfortable in the saddle for 5–6 hours. If you can complete a hilly century in under 6 hours, you'll be right at home on day two.
Can I bring my own bike? What about bike rental? ▾
Absolutely — most riders bring their own road or gravel bike. The route is 90% paved, so road bikes work fine. We partner with a local bike shop in Woodstock for rentals ($65/day, reserve at checkout). Rental bikes are quality aluminum-frame road bikes with Shimano 105 groupsets.
Where does the tour start and end? ▾
Both days start and finish at the Woodstock Village Green in Woodstock, VT. The nearest airports are Manchester-Boston Regional (MHT, ~2 hrs) and Burlington (BTV, ~1.5 hrs). We recommend arriving Friday evening — a welcome dinner is offered for early arrivals.
What happens if the weather is bad? ▾
Vermont weather in September is variable but generally mild (55–72°F). The tour runs rain or shine — we provide route adjustments if needed. In the rare case of dangerous conditions (thunderstorms, icy roads), we have contingency routes and can shorten days. We will not cancel for light rain.
Is there a group size limit? ▾
Yes — we cap participation at 40 riders to keep the experience intimate. The tour typically sells out by early August. We maintain a waitlist and will notify you immediately if a spot opens up.
What's your cancellation and refund policy? ▾
Cancel 30+ days before the event for a full refund. Cancellations 14–29 days out receive a 50% refund or a full credit toward next year's tour. Within 14 days, we can transfer your registration to another rider but cannot issue a cash refund.
